# Visage Aesthetics — Local Growth & Sales Playbook

This is the local engine that turns awareness into bookings. It runs alongside press outreach. Everything here is organic unless you approve spend. Work through it in order of impact: Google Business Profile and reviews first, because that is where most local clinic bookings are won or lost.

---

## 1. Google Business Profile (highest priority)

A polished, active Google Business Profile is the single biggest local lever for a clinic. You have edit access, so we can keep it strong.

**Get the basics right and keep them right:**
- Business name exactly "Visage Aesthetics". Correct category (Medical spa / Skin care clinic / Aesthetics) and any relevant secondary categories.
- Address, opening hours, phone (07931 395246) and website all correct and matching the site.
- Booking link set to the in-house booking page (https://www.vaclinic.co.uk/book-online) so people can book straight from Google. (Must not point at the old Ovatu link.)
- Services list filled out with the real treatments and short descriptions.
- A proper "from the business" description in the Visage voice, citing the award in full.

**Photos:**
- Add real, high-quality photos: the clinic exterior on Friars Lane, the treatment room, Bernadette, and any approved before-and-after images. Refresh monthly. Profiles with recent photos look alive and rank better locally.

**Google Posts (aim for one a week):**
- Short updates: the award, a seasonal offer or tip, a new treatment, a patient win (with consent), a safety tip. Each ends with "Book a consultation" linked to the in-house booking page (vaclinic.co.uk/book-online).
- These are easy wins and keep the profile active. Drafts come from the story angles.

**Q&A:**
- Seed the questions real patients ask (parking, is it nurse-led, does it hurt, do I need a consultation first) and answer them in the Visage voice. Monitor for new questions and answer promptly.

## 2. Reviews (the trust engine)

Reviews are the deciding factor for nervous first-timers. The aim is a steady, genuine flow, never anything bought or faked.

**Generate them:**
- Ask every happy patient at the end of their appointment, in person, and follow up with a friendly message containing the direct Google review link.
- Make it effortless: a short link or QR code on a card at reception and in follow-up messages.
- A gentle WhatsApp follow-up a day or two after treatment works well, when the patient is pleased with how they look.

**Respond to all of them:**
- Reply to every review, positive or negative, in the Visage voice. Thank people warmly, never clinically. For any critical review, respond calmly, take it offline, and never discuss a patient's treatment details in public.

**Use them:**
- The website already pulls through Google reviews. Keep the on-site review set fresh (see the reviews refresh routine in the repo's AGENTS.md).

## 3. Local SEO

The website is already strong on local pages (treatment-by-town pages exist). Keep building local relevance.

- **Consistency (NAP):** Name, address and phone identical everywhere online: website, Google, Instagram, Facebook, and any directory. Inconsistency hurts local ranking.
- **Local directories:** Claim and complete genuine, reputable listings (for example a local business directory, relevant aesthetics directories). Only real, useful ones. No spammy link schemes.
- **Content:** Keep the blog ticking over with genuinely useful local and treatment content. The repo already automates a blog. Feed it the safety-education and seasonal angles.
- **Backlinks the honest way:** Local press features, award listings, partner websites and genuine community involvement all create natural local links. PR and partnerships do double duty here.
- See the repo's SEO_PLAYBOOK.md for the technical detail already in place.

## 4. Instagram and Facebook (local-first)

The audience is local, so the content is local. Instagram is @visageaestheticclinic. Set up or confirm a linked Facebook page for local reach and reviews.

**Content pillars (rotate them):**
1. **Reassurance and education.** Short, calm explainers from Bernadette. The "what to ask before you book" material works perfectly here.
2. **Real results.** Subtle before-and-afters, only with consent.
3. **The human behind the clinic.** Bernadette, the clinic, the care. People book people.
4. **Social proof.** Reviews and the award.
5. **Seasonal and local.** Tie posts to the season and to Braintree and Essex life.

**Practical:**
- Post consistently rather than constantly. Two or three good posts a week beats a daily scramble.
- Use local hashtags and location tags (Braintree, Essex towns).
- Reply to every comment and DM promptly and warmly. DMs are bookings waiting to happen.
- Stories for behind-the-scenes and quick tips. Save the best as highlights (Award, Reviews, Treatments, FAQs).

## 5. Local partnerships

Local businesses that serve the same customers are the warmest route to new patients. Aim for genuine, mutual relationships, not one-off deals.

**Targets:**
- Hair salons and barbers, beauticians and nail bars (non-competing), gyms and personal trainers, wedding venues and bridal suppliers, dentists, boutiques, and other respected local businesses.

**Models:**
- Mutual referrals (you send people their way, they send people yours).
- A small display of cards or a QR code in each other's spaces.
- Joint events or talks (see below).
- Wedding venue partnerships for the bridal angle.

**Approach:** Warm, in person where possible, one at a time. You approve any deal that involves money or a firm commitment. The partnership outreach template is in [outreach-templates.md](outreach-templates.md).

## 6. Referral scheme

Existing happy patients are the best source of new ones.

- A simple, clear scheme: an existing patient refers a friend, and both get a defined thank-you (for example a set amount off a future treatment). Keep it easy to understand and easy to redeem.
- Promote it gently at the end of appointments, in follow-up messages, and occasionally on social.
- Track referrals so the scheme can be measured and the best referrers thanked properly.
- Any discount or reward level is a spend decision, so you sign it off before it launches.

## 7. Community events

Local presence builds local trust.

- **Talks and Q&As:** A short "what to ask before you book" evening, hosted at the clinic or with a partner (a gym, a boutique, a wedding venue). Low cost, high trust.
- **Local events and fairs:** A presence at relevant local markets, wellbeing days or wedding fairs.
- **Charity and community:** Genuine local involvement that fits the brand. Honest, not performative.
- Each event needs your sign-off on any cost and date.

## 8. Seasonal campaigns

Plan the year around natural local moments. Each campaign pulls a story angle, a set of social posts, a Google Post, and where relevant a partner activation.

- **January:** New year, fresh start. Skin health, men's aesthetics, the year ahead.
- **February to May:** Wedding season build-up. Bridal timelines and consultations.
- **April to June:** Summer skin.
- **September to November:** Party season prep.
- **December:** Gifting and thank-yous to existing patients, plus a quiet, warm wrap-up.

## 9. The measurement loop

Keep it simple and honest. Track monthly:
- New enquiries and where they came from (ask every enquirer "how did you hear about us").
- Bookings and consultations.
- New reviews and the live Google rating.
- Press features landed and partnerships started.
- Instagram and Facebook growth and engagement.

Review the numbers monthly, double down on what is working, and quietly drop what is not. The tracker holds outreach status. Booking and enquiry numbers come from the clinic's own systems, which need your access.
