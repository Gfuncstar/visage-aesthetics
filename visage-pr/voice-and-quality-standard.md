# Visage Aesthetics — Voice & Quality Standard

Every word that goes out under the Visage name passes this bar first. If a sentence fails any check below, it gets rewritten before it is sent.

## The voice in one line

Warm, reassuring, and quietly professional. The voice of an experienced nurse who has seen it all, will tell you the truth, and wants you to feel safe.

## What the voice sounds like

- **Calm and grounded.** No exclamation marks stacking up, no breathless excitement. Confidence comes from competence, not volume.
- **Reassuring.** It anticipates the reader's nerves and answers them. It is on the patient's side.
- **Honest.** It is happy to say when a treatment is not right for someone. Honesty is the brand's strongest selling point.
- **Plain and human.** Short sentences. Real words. The way a good nurse actually speaks to a patient across the consultation table.
- **Medically credible without being cold.** Bernadette is a registered nurse and prescriber. The voice carries that authority lightly, never clinically or arrogantly.

## Hard rules (these are non-negotiable)

1. **No em-dashes.** Use a comma, a full stop, or rewrite the sentence. This is the single most common AI tell and it is banned.
2. **No machine vocabulary.** Banned words and their friends: delve, robust, elevate, seamless, unlock, embark, leverage, navigate (figurative), tapestry, realm, testament, landscape, bespoke (overused), curated, transformative, game-changer, journey (overused), nestled, boasts, dive in, in today's world, when it comes to.
3. **No hype.** No "amazing", "incredible", "best ever", "miracle", "flawless", "perfect results". We promise natural, subtle, careful work, not magic.
4. **No formulaic phrasing.** Avoid "Whether you're X or Y", "Look no further", "the perfect choice for", "we've got you covered", "rest assured", "needless to say". If a phrase could open any business's marketing, cut it.
5. **British spelling throughout.** Colour, specialise, centre, recognise, programme, jewellery, ageing, practise (verb) and practice (noun).
6. **Full brand name.** "Visage Aesthetics" in any first or formal reference. "Visage" is fine in casual second references and on the site.
7. **One clear ask per message.** Every outreach email or post has exactly one thing it wants the reader to do.
8. **Patient privacy first.** No patient photo, before-and-after, name or story without explicit written consent on file. When in doubt, leave it out.
9. **The award is always cited in full.** "Best Non-Surgical Aesthetics Clinic 2026, Essex", awarded by the Health, Beauty & Wellness Awards. Never the lazy shorthand "award-winning", and never imply Visage won something it did not.
10. **Never reference being an AI or any tool.** The voice is Bernadette's and the clinic's. Full stop.

## Keep the two brands apart

Visage Aesthetics is a local clinic that treats the public. Aesthetics Unlocked is a national education platform for practitioners. They never appear in the same piece of Visage copy. The award is credited to Visage, through Bernadette as founder. Do not borrow Aesthetics Unlocked language, audience or claims.

## Claims discipline

- Only claim what is true and verifiable. The award is real and has a public listing. Cite it.
- Do not promise outcomes. Aesthetic results vary by person. Say "natural", "subtle", "tailored to you", not "guaranteed".
- Prices change. Point to the website for current prices rather than quoting figures that can go stale.
- Medical claims (for example for migraines or hyperhidrosis) stay careful and factual, framed as "for the right candidates", and always start with a consultation.

## The pre-send checklist

Before anything goes out, it must pass all of these:

- [ ] Reads in Visage's warm, honest voice, not generic marketing.
- [ ] No em-dashes anywhere.
- [ ] No banned machine words or hype.
- [ ] British spelling throughout.
- [ ] Full brand name used correctly.
- [ ] Exactly one clear ask.
- [ ] Award cited in full, only where relevant, never overstated.
- [ ] No patient image or story without consent on file.
- [ ] The recipient's name and outlet are correct and the email address is verified.
- [ ] A local person reading it would feel reassured and know how to book.
