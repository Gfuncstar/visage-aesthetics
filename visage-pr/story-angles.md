# Visage Aesthetics — Local Story Angles

These are the consumer-facing angles we pitch to local Essex media and use across social and the website. Each one is built for a local reader, carries genuine proof, and points to one clear action: book a consultation at Visage Aesthetics. Pick the angle that fits the outlet and the season, then personalise the pitch using the templates in [outreach-templates.md](outreach-templates.md).

Every angle obeys the voice and quality standard. Patient stories are used only with written consent on file.

---

## Angle 1 — The award win (the headline asset)

- **Hook:** A small Braintree clinic has been named Best Non-Surgical Aesthetics Clinic 2026, Essex.
- **Why local media care:** Local-business-does-well is a reliable good-news story for county and town press. It is specific, recent, and tied to a named place and person.
- **The proof:** The award from the Health, Beauty & Wellness Awards, with a public listing at lux-life.digital/winners/vaclinic. Founder Bernadette Tobin available for interview.
- **The angle for the reader:** What it takes to run a safe, honest aesthetics clinic, and why being nurse-led matters.
- **Call to action:** Consultations can be booked at vaclinic.co.uk.
- **Best for:** Regional and town newspapers, county lifestyle magazines, local radio, what's-on sites.
- **Seasonality:** Evergreen for the year of the award. Lead with this first.

## Angle 2 — Nurse-led and safety-first

- **Hook:** Why a registered nurse thinks too many people book aesthetic treatments without asking the right questions.
- **Why local media care:** Consumer-protection and safety angles travel well. Bernadette's clinical background gives a credible, quotable expert voice on a topic readers worry about.
- **The proof:** Bernadette Tobin, Registered General Nurse, Independent Nurse Prescriber, MSc Advanced Practice.
- **The angle for the reader:** Aesthetics is a largely unregulated field. Here is how to tell whether the person treating you is properly qualified.
- **Call to action:** Book a no-pressure consultation to talk through what is right for you.
- **Best for:** Newspapers (health and consumer desks), radio interviews, lifestyle features.
- **Seasonality:** Evergreen. Strong any time, especially alongside national stories about botched treatments.

## Angle 3 — What to ask before you book (safety education)

- **Hook:** Five questions to ask before you let anyone near your face with a needle.
- **Why local media care:** Practical, listicle-friendly, genuinely useful service journalism. Easy for an editor to say yes to.
- **The proof:** Written by a nurse prescriber, so the advice carries weight.
- **The angle for the reader:** Simple checks anyone can make: who is treating me, what are they qualified in, what product is being used, what happens if something goes wrong, and will I get a real consultation first.
- **Call to action:** At Visage Aesthetics, every treatment starts with a proper consultation. Book one to see the difference.
- **Best for:** Lifestyle magazines, what's-on and community sites, newspaper features, Instagram carousels.
- **Seasonality:** Evergreen.

## Angle 4 — Real patient outcomes (consent required)

- **Hook:** A local woman who was nervous about looking "done" shares her experience.
- **Why local media care:** A relatable human story with a beginning, middle and end, set locally.
- **The proof:** A real patient, with written consent, ideally with before-and-after images that show subtle, natural results.
- **The angle for the reader:** What a careful, natural treatment actually looks and feels like, told by someone like them.
- **Call to action:** Consultations at vaclinic.co.uk.
- **Best for:** Lifestyle magazines, Instagram, the website, local Facebook groups.
- **Seasonality:** Evergreen, strong before seasonal pegs.
- **Hard rule:** Never run this without explicit written consent from the patient for that specific use.

## Angle 5 — Party season ready (seasonal)

- **Hook:** How to look like a fresher version of yourself for the party season, without overdoing it.
- **The angle for the reader:** What can be done in good time before Christmas events, and the treatments that need planning weeks ahead rather than days.
- **Call to action:** Book your consultation now so there is time before December.
- **Best for:** Lifestyle magazines, what's-on sites, Instagram.
- **Seasonality:** Pitch from late September through November.

## Angle 6 — Wedding season (seasonal)

- **Hook:** A nurse's honest advice for brides thinking about aesthetic treatments before the big day.
- **The angle for the reader:** Start early, go subtle, never try something new the week before. What a sensible pre-wedding timeline looks like.
- **Call to action:** Book a bridal consultation in good time.
- **Best for:** Wedding venues and suppliers (partnerships), lifestyle magazines, Instagram.
- **Seasonality:** Pitch from January through to early summer.

## Angle 7 — Summer skin (seasonal)

- **Hook:** Looking after your skin through an Essex summer, from a clinic that treats it for a living.
- **The angle for the reader:** Sun, hydration, and which treatments suit the warmer months and which are better left for autumn.
- **Call to action:** Book a skin consultation.
- **Best for:** Lifestyle and what's-on, Instagram.
- **Seasonality:** Pitch from April through June.

## Angle 8 — Men's aesthetics (niche, growing)

- **Hook:** More Essex men are quietly booking aesthetic treatments. A nurse explains what they actually ask for.
- **The angle for the reader:** Subtle, natural treatments tailored to male features, and why men tend to want results no one can point to.
- **Call to action:** Book a consultation.
- **Best for:** Newspapers (lifestyle), radio, Instagram.
- **Seasonality:** Evergreen, with a peg around Father's Day or the new year.

---

## How to choose

1. If you only run one thing this quarter, run **Angle 1 (the award)**.
2. Pair an evergreen safety angle (2 or 3) with whatever seasonal angle (5, 6 or 7) fits the month.
3. Hold patient-story angles (4) until you have consent and strong, natural before-and-after images.
4. Always end on the same single ask: book a consultation at vaclinic.co.uk.
