# Visage Aesthetics — Standing Brief

This is the operating brief for Visage Aesthetics' local press and marketing operation. It explains what we are trying to do, who we are talking to, how the work runs, and where the boundaries are. It is deliberately separate from anything the sister brand Aesthetics Unlocked does.

## 1. The objective

Build steady local awareness in and around Essex that converts into **clinic bookings**: consultations and treatments. Success is measured in enquiries and booked appointments from local people, supported by a strong Google Business Profile, a growing bank of genuine reviews, and a small number of well-placed local press features.

This is a local clinic operation. It is not national. It is not aimed at practitioners. It does not sell courses. If a piece of work does not help a local person feel safe enough to book a consultation with Visage, it does not belong here.

## 2. Who we are talking to

Local consumers in Braintree and the surrounding Essex towns: Witham, Halstead, Chelmsford, Colchester, Maldon, Great Dunmow, Sudbury and the villages between them. Typically women aged roughly 30 to 60 who want natural, subtle results from someone medically qualified, plus a growing number of men. Many are nervous first-timers who have heard horror stories and want reassurance that they are in safe hands.

The emotional job to be done: "I want to look like a fresher version of myself, and I want to know the person doing it is properly qualified and honest with me."

## 3. The channels (all local)

- Regional and county press, and local lifestyle magazines.
- Local radio and what's-on and community sites.
- Awards, including national clinic awards. Awards are the one exception to the local rule below, because national recognition is a credibility asset we can use in local promotion. Given no budget, we only enter awards that are free.
- Google Business Profile, reviews and local SEO.
- Instagram and Facebook aimed at the local area.
- Local partnerships: salons, gyms, wedding venues, dentists, hairdressers and other local businesses.
- A referral scheme, community events, and seasonal campaigns.
- Local influencers and bloggers with a genuine Essex following.

## 3a. Two scope rules that shape everything

1. **No budget. Free and earned only.** No paid ads, no paid award entries, no paid placements. Anything that costs money to run or to accept stops and comes to Giles first. We get our results from the award we already hold, from earned editorial, and from mobilising existing patients, reviewers and followers.
2. **Advertising and promotion stay local, roughly a 20-mile radius of Braintree (CM7 9BL).** That catchment is Braintree, Witham, Halstead, Great Dunmow, Coggeshall, Kelvedon, Chelmsford, Colchester, Maldon, Sudbury, Saffron Walden, Bishop's Stortford and the villages between, which is also the set of towns the website already targets. We do not promote the clinic to a national consumer audience. National awards are the only thing that reaches wider, and only for the credibility.

## 4. How the work runs

1. **I draft, you approve.** I prepare every pitch, post, application and reply. You approve anything that spends money or makes a firm commitment before it happens.
2. **Outreach is personal and one at a time.** Every pitch is written for that specific outlet or person. Never a CC. Never a mass send. Only to addresses we have verified. It goes from the clinic's own mailbox, info@vaclinic.co.uk, as Bernadette and Visage.
3. **We follow up.** After sending, we check the inbox for replies and bounces and handle them promptly and warmly.
4. **We track everything.** Every target, its status, and any reply lives in the tracker (local CSV plus the live Google Sheet).
5. **Consent before any patient story.** No patient photograph, before-and-after, or personal story is used without explicit written consent on file.

## 5. The cadence

There is no fixed schedule unless you set one up. The intended rhythm is the **RUN PR VISAGE** cycle: each time you run it, we check and answer the inbox, send the next three to five local pitches, refresh the tracker, and you get a short summary. Run it as often as you like. A weekly or twice-weekly rhythm keeps momentum without overwhelming local outlets.

## 6. What gets approved by you

- Any spend: award entry fees, advertising, sponsorships, event costs, paid partnerships or gifting.
- Any firm commitment: agreeing to an interview slot, a column, an event date, a partnership deal, or anything that puts the clinic's name to a promise.
- Any use of a patient's image or story (which also needs that patient's written consent).
- Anything that touches the live website in a way you have not already signed off.

## 7. Honest constraints

- I cannot change DNS, hosting, payment or analytics settings. Those need your logins. I will flag them rather than pretend.
- Sending email needs the clinic mailbox connected here. Until info@vaclinic.co.uk is connected, I prepare drafts and tell you, and you send them.
- I cannot verify outside contacts or award details without web access. If web tools are switched off, I will say so and not invent anything.
- I cannot schedule myself. If you want recurring runs, you set that up once.

## 8. The current picture (as built)

- Clinic and founder facts, the award, treatments and links: see [fact-sheet.md](fact-sheet.md).
- Existing assets: the Best Non-Surgical Aesthetics Clinic 2026, Essex award; a strong Google rating; an active Instagram; and the vaclinic.co.uk website. We treat the award win as the headline asset to build the first wave of local PR around.
- Budget: organic for now. No paid spend without your approval.
- Google Business Profile: editable by the clinic, so posts, photos and review replies are all in scope.

## 9. What good looks like in three months

- A short list of local outlets that know Visage and have either run something or are warm to it.
- One strong award entry submitted, free or approved.
- A visibly more active Google Business Profile and a steady trickle of new reviews.
- Two or three real local partnerships in motion.
- A repeatable weekly habit through the RUN PR VISAGE command.
